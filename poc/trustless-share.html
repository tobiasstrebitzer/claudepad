3 · Receive (decrypt a blob sent to you)

You must be signed in as the recipient (section 1).

What this proves: minting an identity, encrypting to a recipient's public key, and per-recipient tiering (body vs body+secrets) all happen entirely client-side - no server is contacted.
What it deliberately skips (the trustless trade-offs): durable availability/pinning, an inbox to discover what's shared with you, and any expiry / burn / revoke (immutable once public). Identity is stored in localStorage (exportable - and therefore your responsibility to protect). Sender name is self-claimed and unverified. Uses ECDH P-256 → HKDF-SHA256 → AES-256-GCM via WebCrypto.
